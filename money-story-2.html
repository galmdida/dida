Once upon a time there were two brothers.

One was named Guyo and the other Dida.

Guyo and Dida were raised by two hardworking, frugal minded parents. These wonderful parents raised the brothers to understand the importance of saving money, spending wisely, and budgeting.

Guyo and Dida both began receiving an allowance at age 12. They were both given the same amount of money and were taught to save at least 10%, tithe 10% and the rest they could spend.

Dida began small chicken farm at a young age. The majority of his weekends and summers were spent at the farm. He was able to earn a good deal of money from this job and he followed his parents counsel by saving and tithing 10% of his income.

The Teen Years

As Dida grew, so did his social life. He loved to spend his weekends with friends. He enjoyed using his spending money, and because He had saved and tithed, He felt no guilt in spending the rest. Dida and his friends spent the majority of their time together going to movies, eating out at restaurants, and shopping at the mall.

At age 18 Dida got a job. This job provided him with part time hours during the school year and full time during the holidays. He was earning more money now, and was diligent about saving money and preparing for his financial future. Dida was very proud of his bank account, and proud that He had been able to save faithfully during his teen years.

When he graduated from high school he had Kes 300,000 in his savings account.

Guyo

Unlike Dida, Guyo didnâ€™t own a farm during his younger years. He tried to do extra chores around the home and was able to earn some extra money that way.

At age 18 Guyo began working at the same store Dida had worked at. Guyo was also able to earn a steady income with part time hours during the school year and full time during the holidays.

Guyo worked hard at his high school job and enjoyed earning money. He was diligent about tithing 10% of his paychecks, but decided to save even more than the 10% his parents had asked of his. Instead, he saved 70% or more of each paycheck and kept very little for personal spending money.

His social life grew, as most teens did. He chose his friends wisely, and found friends who had similar goals and aspirations as he did. Instead of going to movies and shopping, like Dida and his friends did, they instead chose to spend time at each othis homes and enjoying frugal activities together.

Guyo graduated from high school with Kes 1,600,000 in his banking account. (Remember Dida only had Kes 300,000)

So What Happened?

Two brothers. Raised the exact same way. Given the exact same allowance. Worked at the exact same job, received the exact same paychecks. Yet, Guyo ended up with four times the amount of money that Dida had.

The difference?

Guyo was much wiser in his spending. He chose to participate in frugal activities and understood the value in saving as much money as he could.

Dida, on the other hand, did the bare minimum. He didnâ€™t worry much about the future, and figured as long as He was saving a little bit, he would be fine. Dida didnâ€™t realize the life He COULD have given himself if he had only practiced a little more self control. his vision was not as big as Guyo's.

Spending Habits Matter

All good stories end with a moral, and this one is no different.

The story of Guyo and Dida has taught me many many financial lessons. However, this is one lesson that sticks out to me more than the others.

Guyo and Dida received the EXACT same paychecks, for the EXACT same number of years. Their parents taught them the EXACT same money principles in the EXACT same way. But, Guyo chose to save and save and save, while Dida chose to spend.
